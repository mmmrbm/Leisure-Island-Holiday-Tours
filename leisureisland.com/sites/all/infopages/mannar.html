Mannar

The biggest isle of Sri Lanka, Mannar, is situated in northwest of the country. Size of the isle is about 50Km2. Between Mannar and Pamban island of India, there is a chain of limestone shoals, which is called Palk Straits, indicating land connection long time ago among Sri Lanka and India. The isle is mainly dry and barren; Baobab Trees with big trunk can be commonly seen. Fishing is the main livelihood of the residents of the area. The Portuguese styled, Roman Catholic St. Mary's Church Madhu in Mannar district, with history of 400 years and home to the statue of Our Lady of Madhu, is one of the sacred places of Sri Lankan Catholics. Other significant landmark is Yodha Wewa (Giant's Tank), one of the largest tanks in Sri Lanka, was built by King Dhatusena (459-477). Tiruketeeswaram Hindu Temple located at northern area of Mannar; Dutch built Mannar Fort, bird sanctuary are important tourist attractions in the islet.
