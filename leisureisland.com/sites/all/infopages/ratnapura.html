Ratnapura

Rathnapura, About 100 km south east to Colombo, is also called as City of Gems. Gem mining is the focal trade of the city, Ratnapura hold the most gem deposits of the island, gems such as sapphire, ruby, topaz, cat's eye, moonstone, aquamarine, garnet, amethyst, tourmaline, spinel, alexandrite etc. can be found from gem mines, in fact all three of the world's largest blue sapphires and world's most beautiful and of high quality blue sapphires have come from Rathnapura. One route to the Sri Pada (Adam's Peak) Mountain starts from here and excursions to Sinharaja rain forest and Udawalawe National Park can be begun from the city too. Other worth to visit places are Diva Guhava (Buddhist cave temple), famous waterfalls like Bopath Ella, Maha Saman Devalaya (temple) and Peter-Paul Church.
