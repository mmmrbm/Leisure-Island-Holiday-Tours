Jaffna

Jaffna is located in the Jaffna peninsula of northern Sri Lanka, 396Km from Colombo. In the colonial era of Portuguese, Dutch and English, Jaffna had been an important administrative city. As a result of Sri Lanka's Civil war started in 1983, Jaffna lost its reputation as a tourist hotspot. After the war ended in 2009, Jaffna has been booming in tourism as well as other areas. The Jaffna lagoon is an excellent place for bird watching as it attracts wide variety of water birds including American flamingoes, ducks, gulls and terns. At the water's edge of the lagoon, there is the second largest existing Fort of the island which was originally built by Portuguese in 1619 and expanded by Dutch. Nallur Temple, Jaffna library, Nainatheeve isle, Casuarina beach and Nagadeepa Buddhist temple, a group of Dagobas situated close together in Kandarodai are other significant must see places in Jaffna.
