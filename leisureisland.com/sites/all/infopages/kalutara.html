Kalutara

Kalutara, approximately 40 Km south to Colombo, is home to sacred Buddhist temple of Kalutara Bodiya. The most recognizable landmark of Kalutara city, the temple's dagoba, is one of a few dagobas that you are permitted to enter in the island, and it is the world's only hollow Buddhist shrine, the interior contains 74 murals of depicting various moments of the Buddha's life. Calido beach, a thin land which runs between Kalu Ganga (Black River) and the Indian Ocean, is another example for popular island beaches. Canoeing and Kayaking in Kalu River becoming popular among tourists as the river provides calm and peaceful environment for eco- tourism. One of the largest caves in Sri Lanka; the Fa Hien cave(450ft) in Bulathsinhala which gives evident of prehistoric inhabitants of the cave and their lifestyles, and Richmond Castle which is also maintain a children's home are another must visit places in Kalutara.
