Willpattu

Willpattu is the largest National park in Sri Lanka located in Northwest coast lowland dry zone. In 1905 it was declared as a sanctuary and then upgraded to National Park in 1930. The unique feature of this park is the existance of natural sand-rimmed water or deprassions that fill with rain water basins. It is one of the top national park among the world for it's Leopard population.
According to the history this park has a historical value too. First recorded King of Sri Lanka Prince Vijaya(543 BC - 505BC) and his followers came from India and landed the place called Kudiramalai is situated in this park.
Best time to visit this park is during the months of February and October. Only around 25% of the park is open for tours due to existance of dense forest and scrubs in other areas.
