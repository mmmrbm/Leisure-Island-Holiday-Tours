Mihinthale

Mihintale, the cradle of Sinhalese Buddhist civilization, is about 1000 feet rock which is located 12 Km east to world heritage site of Anuradhapura. Mihintale is believed to be the place, where The Buddhism was introduced to Sri Lanka in the 3rd BC. According to the history, Arahath Monk Mahinda, the son of the Indian emperor Asoka, met King Devanampiya Thissa of Sri Lanka to preach the doctrine at the Mihintale. On the full moon day of June every year Buddhists pilgrimage to Mihintale to commemorate this event. There are 1840 granite steps leading to the summit. Ruins of a hospital can be noticed at the foot of the mountain and a stone canoe for medical bath has been discovered along with stone inscriptions and urns. Kantacha chetiya, Mihintale museum, ruins of Mihintale monastery, caves, and ponds imply ancient grandeur of the Mihintale.
