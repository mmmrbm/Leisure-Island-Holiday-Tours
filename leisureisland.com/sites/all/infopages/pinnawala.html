Pinnawala

Pinnawala village is famous for its Elephant Orphanage, which gives protection to wild Asian elephants. It is located in kegalle town in sabaragamuwa province. Pinnawala is well-known for having the largest herd of captive elephants in the world. Orphanage was started by Sri Lankan department of wildlife conservation in 1975 for baby elephants who lost or abandon by their mothers. Elephant breeding program started in 1982 and once they reached adulthood, the elephants are donated, sold or released to wild while disabled retain for further care. The female and young elephants in Pinnawala range freely as a herd during the day. Tourist can witness elephants bathing, drinking in the river and bottle feeding of baby elephants born in Pinnawala.
