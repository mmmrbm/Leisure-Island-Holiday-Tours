Kirinda

Kirinda is a small port village located in the southern Sri Lanka, about 245Km from Colombo, adjacent to Kataragama and Yala. Kirinda ancient Buddhist temple which is located on top of a rock, it has 2000 years of history, is one of the biggest attractions in here and other one is two light houses, 12 and 13Km off the shore, which was built on reefs called Great Basses reef and the Little Basses reef. The reason to build the lighthouses was that coastal area around kirinda contains lots of rocks, the main shipping route that connecting east to west run along Sri Lankan coast, and many ships that had ruined; has made this coastal area a popular scuba diving destination. Another famous landmark is the statue of Queen Viharamaha Devi, Mother of the Sri Lanka's greatest king Dutugemunu.
