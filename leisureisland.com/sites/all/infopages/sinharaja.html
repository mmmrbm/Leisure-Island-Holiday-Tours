Sinharaja

One out of eight UNESCO World Heritage sites of Sri Lanka, The Sinharaja Rain Forest, is located in Sabaragamuwa and Southern Province.The reserve is 21 Km from east to west, and 7 Km from north to south. This dense virgin rainforest is a biodiversity hotspot with many endemic fauna and flora. Prominent characteristic of the forest is the density of trees and diversity of the species. Out of 211 trees and lianas within the reserve, 139 are endemic. Many species of ferns, epiphytes, generas and shrubs add more value to this calm, dark, wet, mysterious rain forest. Although elephants, deers and leopards rarely can be seen in the forest, The Purple-faced Monkey is the common larger mammal in here. Butterflies, Amphibians, Reptiles, various type of colorful flock of birds and fish use this majestic ecosystem as their habitats.
