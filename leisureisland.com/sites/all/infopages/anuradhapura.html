Anuradhapura

It is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, Anuradhapura, one of the eight UNESCO World Heritage Site of the island which is about 200 Km north east to Colombo. This city is very important to Buddhists as it bears the Eight Great Places of Veneration (Atamasthana). They are; The Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi(sacred fig ) tree - It is said to be the southern branch from the historical Bodhi tree Sri Maha Bodhi at Bodh Gaya in India under which Lord Buddha attained Enlightenment, The Ruwanwelisaya - an architectural wonder of 338 feet, is a Buddhist stupa( temple ) which was built in 140 B.C by Great King Dutugemunu, Thuparamaya - It is considered to be the first dagoba (temple) built in Sri Lanka following the introduction of Buddhism, Lovamahapaya - It is also known as the Brazen Palace because the roof was covered with bronze tiles, that is ruined now and stone pillars can be seen, Abghayagiri Dagoba - It was a great monastic centre and one of the most extensive ruins in the world, Jetavanaramaya - It is one of the tallest structures in the ancient world with height of 400 feet, at the time of its completion the structure was the third tallest structure in the world behind the Great Pyramids of Giza, Mirisaveti Stupa - It was built by King Dutugemunu after placing the Buddha relics in the sceptre, Lankarama Dagoba - Rows of stone pillars around the stupa is giving evidence that it was encircled by some kind of a building. Other places to visit are isurumuniya, Magul Uyana, Kuttam Pokuna (pond), Rathna Prasadaya, Samadhi Statue and Archaeological Museum.
