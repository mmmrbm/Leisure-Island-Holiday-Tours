Adam's Peak

Well know name is The Sri Pada, mean sacred footprint, a 7359 ft. tall Mountain located in Rathnapura District of Sabaragamuwa Province of Sri Lanka. The Mountain and adjacent area is a wild reserve with many endemic species. Sri Pada is another significant sacred site for Buddhists as they believe the indentation resembling a footprint at the summit belongs to Lord Buddha. The pilgrimage season start in December full moon to May full moon. Climbing the mountain in off season is bit difficult because of rain and mist but it would be an adventurous journey for thrill seekers to do so. Watching the sunrise from top of the mountain is another target of pilgrims and tourists. Breathtaking views and enchanting forested sanctuary create a perfect Eco-tourism destination for the island.
